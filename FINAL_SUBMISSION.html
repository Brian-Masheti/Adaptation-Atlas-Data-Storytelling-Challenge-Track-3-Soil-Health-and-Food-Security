Introduction

Sub-Saharan Africa faces unprecedented soil health challenges that directly threaten food security for millions of people. Climate change intensifies soil degradation through increased temperatures, erratic rainfall patterns, and extreme weather events. This comprehensive analysis utilizes advanced deep learning techniques, including Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, to identify critical soil health risks, project future impacts, and propose innovative technology solutions for sustainable agriculture.

Our methodology integrates multiple world-class datasets including Adaptation Atlas hazard data, SoilGrids soil information, CHC-CMIP6 climate projections, TerraClimate environmental metrics, and ESA satellite observations. By employing ensemble deep learning models with rigorous validation, we provide actionable intelligence for soil health management and food security planning.
